# v0.5 → v1+ → long-term build plan

The phasing for Dome over time. v0.5 is the demo; v1+ is the product; long-term is what the product becomes when it works.

## v0.5 — Demo

**Goal:** Prove the four-concept core compiles in real use. The author is the first user.

**Ships:**

- TypeScript SDK on Bun. Single npm package.
- The four concepts: Vault, Document, Tool, Hook ([[wiki/specs/sdk-surface]]).
- 7 Tools: readDocument, writeDocument, appendLog, searchIndex, wikilinkResolve, moveDocument, deleteDocument.
- Named invariants across the axiom / shipped-default / opt-in tiers; most enforced at Tool boundary, plus `VAULT_IS_GIT_REPO` enforced at vault-open boundary. Canonical list: `docs/wiki/invariants/`.
- Hook system: programmatic + declarative forms; async by default; cycle detection; drop-zone intake pattern.
- Prompt library: the shipped workflow prompts (canonical list: [[wiki/specs/prompts-and-workflows]] §"Shipped workflows by tier") + `system-base.md`.
- MCP server: one MCP tool per SDK tool; works with Claude Code, Cursor, OpenCode, Codex CLI.
- CLI: the shipped command surface (canonical list: [[wiki/specs/cli]] §"Implementation note").
- Eval suite shape (fixtures + expected effects).

**Harnesses:**

- Claude Code (primary; the author's daily driver).
- Other MCP-capable agents (Cursor, OpenCode, Codex) work by adding one config line.

**Browser:** Obsidian. The vault is plain markdown.

**Validation:** The author uses Dome for their real `~/vaults/work` and `~/vaults/personal` for ~3 months. Real PMing happens against real workflow friction. Anything that doesn't work gets fixed before v1.

## v1+ — Product

**Goal:** Anyone can use Dome to extend their brain.

**Ships:**

- Native mobile app (iOS first; voice-first capture; structured browse; prep mode; inbox review).
- Native desktop app (electron / tauri shell; same surfaces as mobile + power-user TUI option).
- Optional cloud-synced vault (markdown native; Syncthing or first-party sync service).
- Onboarding flow that meets users where they are: blank vault, Obsidian vault, Apple Notes, Google Docs.
- Polished `dome migrate` (the v0.5 sleeper feature, now the on-ramp).
- Plugin marketplace / discovery surface.
- Voice client (AirPods → inbox/voice → ingest).
- Refined prompts based on v0.5 dogfooding + user-research-driven additions.
- Mobile + desktop apps speak MCP to a local Dome service (Bun's compile-to-binary) OR embed the SDK via FFI.

**Audience:** The wedge expands from the author to high-context operators (managers, founders, researchers, consultants), then broadens. The principles ([[wiki/invariants/MARKDOWN_IS_SOURCE_OF_TRUTH]], [[wiki/concepts/brain-companion]]) are structural; they make Dome work for anyone.

## Long-term — Always-with-you

**Goal:** Dome is what people use the way they use their phones.

The vision (from [[VISION]] §"Long term"): ambient, always there, low-friction. You think out loud; Dome remembers. You ask; Dome answers from your own thinking. The garden grows over years because the gardening is automatic. The cognitive surface of an individual — what they pay attention to, what they decide, what they change their mind about — becomes a durable, exportable, queryable asset that compounds for a lifetime.

What this requires beyond v1:

- Cross-device sync that handles concurrent writes well ([[wiki/gotchas/concurrent-harness-write]] at scale).
- Temporal awareness — "what was I thinking 6 weeks ago"; the v1+ atomic-claim-event-log if needed.
- Native voice capture at the OS level (iOS / macOS dictation deeply integrated).
- An ecosystem of plugins for different domains (writers, researchers, family historians, founders).
- The market trusting that Dome's local-first / markdown-canonical promise will hold over years.

The long-term vision is a north star, not a 2026 product spec. It guides v1+ feature decisions: anything that adds proprietary lock-in or breaks the markdown-canonical contract is out of bounds, even if it would ship faster.
