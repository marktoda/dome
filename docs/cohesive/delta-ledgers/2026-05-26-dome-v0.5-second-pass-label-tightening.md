## Delta at a glance

**Classification:** **Mixed.** Design-layer changes: (a) `ConsumerSurface` (one MCP-bound aggregation) splits into `AbstractSurface` (protocol-agnostic, in `@dome/sdk` core) + per-protocol renderers (`renderMcp` in `@dome/sdk/mcp`, future `renderHttp` / `renderVoice` in their companion entrypoints); (b) new axiom `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND` (15th invariant) pins the single-source helper `wrapMutatingInvoke`; (c) `WORKFLOWS_KNOW_VAULT_CONTEXT` extension model rewritten to honestly name "SDK-internal in v0.5; plugin/vault preamble registration deferred to v0.5.1+"; (d) `projectMcp(vault)` and `buildToolAdapters(vault)` collapse — protocol rendering happens inside `renderMcp(surface)` consuming `surface.tools` rather than re-binding; (e) `McpToolName` PascalCase alias deleted (closes pass-1 F7 remnant); (f) bare-event-expansion rule (`event: document.written` → `document.written.*`) documented in `hooks.md` and the event-types matrix. Implementation-layer changes are deferred to `implement-cohesively`: the type/factory introductions, the helper extraction, the call-site updates, six new/extended tests, deletions.

**Files:** 2 substrate files added (1 invariant, 1 deferred linter spec). 9 substrate files rewritten (4 specs, 3 matrices, 1 invariant, 1 catalog index). Tests are listed under §"Implementation work" and not in the added-files count.

**Conceptual changes:** The **AbstractSurface + render-Xxx pattern** is the structural realization of "labels on the new seam match the structure." Pre-pass-2, `ConsumerSurface` was named protocol-agnostic but its `tools: ReadonlyArray<ToolAdapter>` field was MCP-rendered, and the matrix already admitted one ConsumerSurface per protocol. Post-pass-2, `AbstractSurface.tools: BoundToolSurface` is the protocol-agnostic shape (identical to `vault.tools`); `McpSurface.tools: ReadonlyArray<ToolAdapter>` is the MCP-rendered shape produced by `renderMcp(surface)`. A v1+ HTTP / voice / desktop shell ships its protocol-renderer in a companion entrypoint and reuses `buildAbstractSurface` unchanged. **`HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND`** is the substrate-shape pin that prevents the pre-pass-2 duplication (two implementations of the intrinsic-wrap, one in `bindEntry` and one in `bindAiSdkTools`) from reappearing: the wrap is a single-source helper consumed by every projection, and the AC3-lockstep counterpart is a pair of integration tests covering the two v0.5-shipped projections (MCP + AI-SDK).

**Named invariants:** `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND` (axiom, added). End-state total per the canonical const after `code-invariants-const` lands: 9 axiom + 4 shipped-default + 2 opt-in = 15 (the substrate catalog at `docs/index.md` already lists 15; the `INVARIANTS` const at `src/types.ts` ships v0.5 with 13 entries and gains both `WORKFLOWS_KNOW_VAULT_CONTEXT` and `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND` in `implement-cohesively`).

**Behavior matrices:** `consumer-surface.md` rewritten — `MCP adapters` column becomes `Protocol renderer`; `ConsumerSurface` column becomes `AbstractSurface`; Tools column cells normalize (MCP server's Tools cell becomes `core` because `surface.tools` IS `vault.tools`; protocol rendering moves into the Protocol-renderer column); future-HTTP / future-voice rows reference `core` + `future-http` / `future-voice` renderers. `tool-invariant-enforcement.md` gains two new off-matrix sections (`WORKFLOWS_KNOW_VAULT_CONTEXT` — runner-enforced; `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND` — projection-enforced) and updates the off-matrix list from three invariants to five. `event-types-and-payloads.md` gains §"Expansion convention" mirroring the bare-event rewrite rule.

**Gotchas:** No new gotchas in this rewrite. Pass-2 F8 cluster (`vault-bootstrap-temporal-dead-zone`, `post-close-tool-call`, `canonical-string-enum-shape`) is deferred to Phase C per the review's roadmap.

**Semantic linters:** One deferred linter spec added: [[wiki/linters/wrap-mutating-invoke-consumption]] (v0.5.1+). v0.5 ships the spec but not the implementation — the structural enforcement that ships is the single-source helper `wrapMutatingInvoke` + the two hook-dispatch integration tests; the linter spec carries against the byte-equivalent-hand-inlined-duplicate gap those structures cannot close.
