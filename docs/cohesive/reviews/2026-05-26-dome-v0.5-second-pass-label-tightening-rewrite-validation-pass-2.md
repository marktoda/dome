## Architectural reflection

How it feels now: The two-layer split (`AbstractSurface` in core; `renderMcp`/future `renderHttp`/`renderVoice` in companion entrypoints) makes the structural fact match the labels — `surface.tools` IS `vault.tools`, protocol shaping happens only in the renderer, and the new `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND` axiom pins this with a single-source helper. The seven-Tool seal now compounds through three substrate joins (registry → bound surface → projection) without losing legibility; a future contributor reading `sdk-surface.md` §"Consumer surfaces" then `consumer-surface.md` can trace any consumer to its entrypoint set without reading code. The substrate is consistent and the labels carry their weight.

- **Easier downstream:** Adding `@dome/sdk/http` is now a ~50-line `renderHttp(surface)` + an integration test, not a reimplementation of the four-kind aggregation; the matrix already shows the `future-http` cells where the work lands.
- **Harder downstream:** The wrap is enforced by two integration tests + reviewer attention until v0.5.1+'s linter ships. A third projection landing before the linter is the brittle moment — the substrate honestly names this in `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND.md`'s §"Structural enforcement" + §"Counter-example".
- **Load-bearing on memory:** The "every projection ships its own hook-dispatch integration test" convention is enforced by reviewer attention pre-linter. The deferred `workflow-cli-preamble-coverage.test.ts` lockstep is similarly memory-borne pre-fourth-workflow.

### I2. `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND.md` counter-example uses retired `projectXxx` naming

**Severity:** Low
**Category:** Spec drift

**Why it matters:** Pass-1 B3 normalized the canonical speculative-renderer set to `renderHttp` / `renderVoice` across four surfaces. The new axiom's prose at the duplication-explanation paragraph names hypothetical future projections as `projectHttp`, `projectGrpc`, `projectIpc` — the pre-split `projectXxx` convention. The line below (counter-example) correctly uses `renderHttp`. A future contributor reading the axiom doc could think "do I name my new projection `projectFoo` or `renderFoo`?"

**Evidence:** `docs/wiki/invariants/HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND.md` §"Why" — "a future contributor adding a third projection (`projectHttp`, `projectGrpc`, `projectIpc`) consumes the helper" vs the next-paragraph counter-example — "a v1+ contributor adds `@dome/sdk/http` with `renderHttp(surface): HttpSurface`".

**Recommended fix:** Rewrite the duplication paragraph to use the canonical naming: "a future contributor adding a third projection (`renderHttp`, `renderVoice`, or similar protocol renderer)". Drop `projectGrpc` and `projectIpc` — they were B3-retired.

**Substrate artifact to add or update:** `HOOK_DISPATCH_IS_VAULT_BOUND.md` §"Why".
